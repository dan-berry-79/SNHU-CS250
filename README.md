## Reflection
This course helped me better understand how user needs are translated into software requirements throught the use of user stories. User stories provide a structured way to identify customer needs and prioritize features that deliver value. Throughout the SNHU Travel project, I learned how Agile practices allow requirements to evolve while keeping development aligned with customer expectations.

My approach to software development now includes breaking projects into smaller tasks, gathering feedback throughout development, and using iterative improvement rather than waiting until the end of a project to make changes. Agile practices such as sprint planning, sprint reviews, and retrospective helps teams remain adaptable and continuously improve their processes.

Being a good team member in software development means communicating effectively, collaborating with others, and remaining open to feedback. Successful teams depend on trust, accountability, and a shared commitment to delivering value to customers. The Scrum roles used throughout this course demonstrated how each team member contributes to the overall success of a project.
